Tackling COVID with a optimum technique

As the times are going tough, contact itself has become one of the most feared action between human beings. This not just restricts our day to day interaction to certain levels but also comes in the way of diagnosis of the very disease that caused this. As we know, the age old methods of measuring heart rate and oxygen saturation levels are highly dependent on touching the person/victim. This can result in spreading of virus even more.

Health Risks

In a lot of sectors, employers don't want to risk the spread of virus even more, thus they are being pulled away from there workers, this is specially true in developing countries with a lot of workers coming from rural areas. This has made a lot of these workers abandon there jobs and lively hood without money. This not just affects the personal bussiness but also the economy of the sector in general. A simple, automatic way to verify employee health is needed to improve logistics and ensure a much safer way of detection of any such contagious diseases.

SOLUTION!

Yes, we bring to you an easy way that can be path breaking in the health domain

The Solution we propose will give the robot the ability to extract important information such as heart rate, elevated body temperature and most importantly , SPo2 level from the video of their faces. Regular check ups of workers can be scheduled in daily basis and if any of the above three go in the direction of being a symptom of covid-19, the manager or the worker can be alerted before hand.

Working of this estimation

The algorithm works by extracting Region of interest from the person's face. The RGB values are extracted pixel by pixel using skin detection. The green channel is sent under various FFT and arithmetic calculations to print out the frequency of blood being pumped out from the heart. This frequency is taken into threshold of certain limit to extract heart rate. The range of error is around 3.08% but sometimes due to lighting issues it can fluctuate.

For the estimation of Spo2 value from face, we extract the mean red and blue value of each frame. This mean value is then fed into a formula which was derived by a study conducted in MIT. The formula can be listen as, extracting the DC and AC value and using wavelength of red and infrared lights to extract the Spo2 values. Formula : SpO2 = A - B*(Iac/Idc)
The Flashback

Our journey exploring this mystery element SPO2!

The proposed idea is currently halfway through development. With the support of various local hospital and health care centres, we are working on getting the data and working on our algorithm. The error range in our algorithm right now is between 2-5 per cent for spo2 estimation, with the ground truth value being the finger spo2 pulse oximeter. The whole device is a plug and play, it can be used along with the robot as a whole or bought differently as a stand-alone product in itself. The proposed idea was presented in MIT's COVID-19 challenge where it took the first spot and was supported by various judges and members after the competition.
